History of Ohanaeze Ndigbo

Ohanaeze Ndigbo Worldwide is the umbrella body for all Igbo socio-cultural organizations, communities, and individuals of Igbo origin at home and in the diaspora.

Formed in 1976, the organization emerged from a vital collective need to restore absolute synergy, cultural pride, and geopolitical positioning for the Igbo people globally following historical challenges.

The groundwork for the organization was initiated by iconic Igbo leaders, legal luminaries, and traditional rulers who envisioned a platform devoid of political factionalism. Its foundational philosophy rests on "Igwebuike" (strength in numbers) and "Onye aghana nwanne ya" (never leave a brother behind).

Over the decades, Ohanaeze Ndigbo has evolved from an elite advisory council into a powerful socio-cultural movement. Today, it leads strategic initiatives in maternal language preservation, local economic restoration, educational grants, and historical defense of the rights of Igbo communities worldwide.

Framework

Organizational Structure

Ohanaeze Ndigbo operations run on a structured framework built to ensure grassroot, state, national, and international inclusion of all Igbos across the globe.

1

Ime-Obi (The Inner Circle)

The highest advisory body within the organization. It is comprised of elder statesmen, former political leaders, royal fathers, and seasoned intellectuals who guide policy directions and critical decision-making processes.

2

General Assembly

The apex legislative organ of Ohanaeze Ndigbo. This broad assembly hosts all segments of Igbo society to elect executive officers, debate central motions, and rectify vital constitutional changes.

3

National Executive Committee (NEC)

Led by the President-General, the NEC supervises day-to-day administrative affairs and executes resolutions passed by the Ime-Obi and the General Assembly.

4

Youth and Women Wings

Specialized organs tasked with mobilizing, mentoring, and advancing programs that empower Igbo youth and women across political, leadership, and socioeconomic spheres.

5

State & Diaspora Chapters

Structured across Nigerian states and specialized international branches to ensure Ndigbo anywhere globally remain tied to their rich cultural root.

Governance Framework

Constitution and By-laws

Supreme Constitution

The activities of Ohanaeze Ndigbo Worldwide are strictly governed by a supreme written constitution. This document ensures democratic rotation of key executive roles among the seven core Igbo-speaking states (Abia, Anambra, Ebonyi, Enugu, Imo, Delta, and Rivers) to preserve parity, stability, and enduring brotherhood.

Live Constitution Document
PREAMBLE

We, the people of Ndigbo, firmly resolved to establish a socio-cultural organization to preserve our heritage, protect our common interests, and foster global unity, do hereby enact and give to ourselves this Constitution...

ARTICLE 1: NAME AND SUPREMACITY

1.1 The name of the organization shall be OHANAEZE NDIGBO WORLDWIDE.
1.2 This Constitution shall be supreme, and its provisions shall guide all national, state, and diaspora operations cleanly.

ARTICLE 2: ROTATION POLICY

2.1 The office of the President-General shall rotate democratically among the seven recognized Igbo-speaking state chapters: Abia, Anambra, Ebonyi, Enugu, Imo, Delta, and Rivers State sequentially.

ARTICLE 3: AMENDMENTS

3.1 No clause of this document shall be altered except by a two-thirds majority vote of sitting members during an officially convened General Assembly meeting.
